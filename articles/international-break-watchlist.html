International Break Watchlist

September 4, 2019

Tammy Abraham has found the net 4 times in his last 2 matches

After an exciting 4 weeks, the first international break of the Premier League season is finally among us. With many managers contemplating different changes to their sides, let’s examine 5 options who could make your team in the near future.

1. Tammy Abraham

Despite Chelsea’s slow start to the season, picking up only 5 points and 1 win from their matches so far, Abraham has been one of the bright spots, bagging 2 braces in his last 2 matches. Looking to beat out the competition for Chelsea’s starting striker, it seems certain that Abraham has secured his place for now as Chelsea’s #1.

With this in mind, he has 2 tricky fixtures, one away to Wolves and one hosting Liverpool at Stamford Bridge, before facing an easier run of fixtures that includes Brighton and Newcastle at home. So far, Abraham’s attacking statistics seem promising, as he has managed to get off 2.8 shots per game and make 2.1 key passes per game. If Abraham can continue his run of form into Chelsea’s easier fixtures, he can undoubtably repay mangers bold enough to take a risk on him.

Verdict: Worth picking up after GW6 as long as he continues to start every match.

Todd Cantwell has started all 4 matches for Norwich this season

2. Todd Cantwell

One of the biggest surprises of the season so far, Cantwell is undoubtably one of the best budget players so far this season. Originally priced at only 4.5, and now priced at 4.8, Cantwell and teammate Teemu Pukki have helped Norwich get off to a positive start after their return to the Premier League. Although Norwich had difficult fixtures against Liverpool and Chelsea, they have managed to score in 3 of their first 4 games. Cantwell so far has found one goal and 2 assists, and has shown promise in many of his stats such as his average of 2 key passes per game and 1 shot per game.

Although Cantwell won’t bring in double digit points every week, he is still one to watch due to his low price point.

Verdict: Worth picking up due to his great value and attacking prospects.

Aubameyang has found points in each of his first 4 matches

3. Pierre-Emerick Aubameyang

Auba is off to a reasonable start this season after finding returns in each of his first 4 matches. Despite this, he is yet to register double digit points, but he may be set to do so with a good run of upcoming fixtures. Between gameweek 5 and gameweek 16, Aubameyang faces just 1 top 6 side, Manchester United, in gameweek 7. Over this period, Auba could find a large portion of his points for this season, especially considering he looks to be a better option than Harry Kane, who is priced similarly.

Despite this, many of the top players this season so far have been midfielders, and no strikers have emerged as must-owns thus far. With his good run of opponents, Auba could become the standout option before his fixtures turn for the worse near the festive period.

Verdict: A risky gamble, but one that can pay off.

Dan James has found his way into the Manchester United XI

4. Dan James

Another breakout star so far this season is Daniel James. Acquired from Swansea City for $15 million, James has scored 3 times so far this season, and has been one of the bright spots in a defunct Manchester United XI. James has been creating the space to shoot from the wing, as he has averaged 2.8 shots per game so far. With Martial and Rashford yet to make a large impact as goalscorers, James could emerge as United’s main man this season. Considering his price is only 6.1, it could be worth the risk to bring him in.

Despite all the promise, it is possible his goalscoring may slow down significantly. While playing for Swansea, James found the net just 4 times in 33 appearances for the club. Manchester United also have some tricky fixtures in their next 5, with matches against Leicester, Arsenal, and Liverpool.

Verdict: Continue watching for now, consider after GW9.

Jamie Vardy has scored the 3rd most goals in 2019

5. Jamie Vardy

Once again, Vardy has proved himself a fantasy option once again this season after picking up 3 goals and an assist in the first 4 matches of the season. Vardy has already proven his ability to excel in the Premier League, as he has found over 150 points in each of his last 4 seasons. So far, he has found returns in his 2 easier matches, but blanked in his more difficult 2. Since the start of 2019, Vardy has scored 15 times, making him one of the top scorers in the Premier League this year.

Vardy has benefitted from Leicester’s change in management, as his improved record coincides with Brendan Rogers’ appointment. In addition, new teammate Youri Tielmans has proved his potential to assist and improve Leicester’s creativity in attack.

Verdict: Consider bringing in GW 6 after matches vs. Manchester United and Tottenham.
