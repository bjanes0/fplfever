Midfielders to Watch

July 26, 2019

Zaha scored 143 points last season

This year we have been given a large number of FPL midfield options from various pricepoints; here are a couple that may not start in your team, but are worth keeping an eye on throughout the early stages of the season.

Ayoze Perez (6.5) looks set to be a top mid-priced option this season following his transfers from Newcastle United to Leicester. Playing out of position as a forward or winger, Perez will be enthusiastic to join up with a fiery Leicester attack who managed 17 goals following Rogers first match in March. In addition, Perez scored 8 for Newcastle in the 899 minutes he played since Roger's takeover at Leicester. Leicester will be enthusiastic for a solid start to the season despite facing 4 of the top 6 in their first 10 matches. Perez, who scored 12 of Newcastle's 42 goals last season is already off to a solid start in preseason, scoring in Leicester's 1-0 victory versus Scunthorpe on the 16th.

Wilfried Zaha (7.0) is another player many managers will be keen on placing in their teams, with the Crystal Palace man currently in 29.2% of teams. He will be looking to carry the momentum from the end of last season after finding 7 of his 10 goals after the new year. However, Palace faces looming defensive issues after losing Aaron Wan-Bissaka (5.5) to Manchester United and injuries to Mamadou Sakho (5.0) and James Tomkins (5.0) could see their results take a downward turn.

Another player worth watching is Kevin de Bruyne (9.5). Available at a discounted price this year following an injury ridden campaign last season, de Bruyne will be looking to continue his form he found late last season before picking up another small injury that left him out the final few weeks. With 965 minutes under his belt last season, de Bruyne found a league high .62 xA/90. If he can carry that momentum into this season, he could be a great option at his mid-high pricepoint.

de Bruyne in action vs. Wolves July 20th

Chelsea midfielders Pedro (7.0), Willian (7.0) and new signing Pulisic (7.5) all make the list as it is currently unclear who will emerge to fill the gap left behind by the departure of Eden Hazard. Pedro found the net far more times than Willian, with 8 goals to Willian's 3, however, Willian's 7 assists topped Pedro's 2. Pulisic on the other hand found the net 4 times and assisted 4 times in 20 appearances for Dortmund last season.

Gylfi Sigurdsson (8.0) will be looking to lead Everton's attack this season after recording the 4th most points for all midfielders with 13 goals and 8 assists. Sigurdsson has the benefit of being on penalty duty once again for the upcoming season, and flanked by Richarlison (8.0) and Theo Walcott (6.5) as well as an improved Domenic Calvert-Lewin (6.0), Sigurdsson will hope his teammates will provide the support he needs to keep the points flowing this season.

Priced at a modest 7.5, David Silva (7.5) could find his way into many teams this season. The Spanish playmaker will be sure to retain his minutes despite City's many signings, and with his lower pricepoint, he could be a simple option to get invested in the Manchester City attack. After racking up 143 points last season and missing only 5 matches, Silva will be looking to add onto his 6 goals and 8 assists last season.
