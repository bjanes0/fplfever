5 Differential Defenders

July 27, 2019

Kyle Walker's price has been reduced by .5 this season

Many fantasy managers this season will be looking to pick up at least two premium defenders such as Andrew Robertson (7.0), Trent Alexander-Arnold (7.0) or Aymeric Laporte (6.5), but will be left with 1 or 2 extra slots for an additional defender. Here are 5 options that can help you get ahead early this season.

Kyle Walker (6.0) leads off the list. Priced at 6.0, he looks like a top option due to his price reduction and consistent minutes. Although few players in Pep's lineups are safe from rotation, Walker should be starting most matches with the occasional rotation with Danilo (5.0). In addition to his consistent minutes, Walker picked up only 1 goal and 1 assist last season, compared to 12 assists in the 2 seasons before. If he can pick up his offensive numbers, Walker could finish in the top 5 point scorers for defenders this season. Selected by 15.7%, Walker could return big points early on as he faces a number of offensively weak sides in his first 10 matches of the season such as Norwich and Brighton.

Another City man, Oleksander Zinchenko (5.5) could make his mark in FPL this season. As the budget City defensive option and in just 5.7% of teams, Zinchenko could provide a budget option into the City defense. At the end of last season, Zinchenko solidified his spot in the title-winning Manchester City side, starting 11 of the final 12 matches of the season. In those games, he kept 9 clean sheets, conceding just twice. In addition, he managed 3 assists, proving his ability to attack. In addition, new signing Angelino (5.0) failed to impress in pre-season and Benjamin Mendy (6.0) looks to miss out on the opening weeks due to injury. Overall, Zinchenko's starting spot should be sealed for the start of the season.

Everton man Seamus Coleman (5.5) will be keen on carrying his run of good form from last season into this one. Coleman kept 6 clean sheets in his final 7 games of the season and was able to find his place in the Everton attack once again, as he scored twice and assisted on two other occasions. Everton proved their return to being a Premier League powerhouse with victories and clean sheets against Manchester United, Chelsea, and Arsenal at the end of last season. With just 3.8% of players owning the Irishman, he seems a good option for anyone looking to make a small downgrade from a 6.0 defender to a slightly cheaper one. 

Another wing-back, Ben Chilwell (5.5), is another differential option at just 6.0% ownership. Chilwell proved his attacking potential last season after finding 5 assists, and has been priced under teammate Ricardo Pereira (6.0). Chilwell could certainly build on his points tally of 118 last season. Leicester proved their defensive capabilities last year as they had the 5th best xGA in the league and the 7th least goals conceded. With a few tricky fixtures at the start of the season, it could be difficult for Chilwell to get off to a good start, but he could certainly be a solid option throughout the season.

Lastly, a bit of a wildcard option is Max Aarons (4.5) of newly promoted Norwich City. Aarons was one of the most attacking wing-backs in the Championship last season after picking up 2 goals and 6 assists, and being consistently involved in the attack. At an appropriate price point, Aarons could make up for Norwich’s potential trouble with attacking returns, due to the high value of attacking defenders in FPL.

Overall, many solid defensive options are yet to be picked up by managers and could give your team a competitive edge. My pick to perform the best is Zinchenko, but I’m excited to see what this season holds for defensive players.
