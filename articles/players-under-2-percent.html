Top Players With Under 2% Ownership

July 28, 2019

Here are some differential players who can give your team a head start this season!

A team composed of players all of whom have less than 2 percent ownership

Goakeepers

Angus Gunn (owned by 1.5%, 4.5)

After emerging as the first-choice keeper for Southampton last season, Gunn looks set to retain his place in net, despite being owned by a small fraction of players. Following Ralph Hasenhüttl’s appointment in December, Southampton managed to avoid relegation and show massive signs of improvement. One of the changes by the Austrian manager was placing Gunn in goal who kept a clean sheet vs. Chelsea in his first appearance of the season. In the following weeks he became the first-choice goalkeeper, keeping 2 clean sheets in the following 11 matches he started. Gunn could also be a good pick while still feeling out the new season, as he faces Burnley, Brighton, and Sheffield all away in the first 5 matches of the season.

Ralf Fährmann (owned by 0.8%, 4.5) –

New signing Fährmann will be ambitious to beat the competition and become the first choice for Norwich this season. Despite being relegation favorites, we have seen in the past goalkeepers for poor defenses managing high points totals, such as Neil Etheridge’s high points total last season. Norwich managed 13 clean sheets in their 46 Championship matches last season and have managed to retain their top defenders. Despite some tricky matches in his first 5, facing Liverpool, Chelsea, and Manchester City, Fährmann could find plenty of save points and emerge the top 4.5 goalkeeper this season.

Defenders

Serge Aurier (owned by 1.4%, 5.0) –

Following the surprising transfer of Kieran Trippier to Atletico Madrid, Aurier could be set to start the season at right back for Spurs. At just 5.0, he would be the cheapest starter in the Tottenham defense who managed 13 clean sheets last season, the 5th most in the league. Despite this, he could face rotation with Kyle Walker-Pieters due to Spurs’ involvement in the Champions League.

Jack O’Connell (owned by 0.8%, 4.5) –

Although being at just 0.8% ownership, O’Connell could be the best option from the Sheffield United defense. Sheffield was undoubtably the best defense in the Championship last season, as they managed a league high 21 clean sheets. O’Connell managed a significant offensive contribution as well, with 3 goals and 3 assists. Priced at only 4.5, rather than 5.0, O’Connell could be one of the top 4.5 defenders this season.

Ben Johnson (owned by 1.2%, 4.0) –

Johnson, the 19 year old right back, has shown a large amount of promise in pre-season and has been handed numerous starts by Hammers manager Manuel Pellegrini. With several impressive performances under his belt already, Johnson could nail down a starting spot in the West Ham starting XI. At just 4.0, the cheapest price for a defender, he could find his way into many top teams if he becomes a starter.

Jan Bednarek (owned by 1.2%, 4.5) –

Like Gunn, Bednarek is a staple of the Southampton defense that showed so much promise at the conclusion of last season. Bednarek, despite earning just 3 bonus points, will be Southampton’s best outfield option due to his threat from set pieces and his promise shown in the bonus point system.

Matthew Lowton (owned by 0.3%, 4.5) –

Rounding out the defenders is Burnley right back Matthew Lowton. Lowton should be set to start at right back once again this season following some trouble with injuries in the past. Lowton has shown his ability to earn FPL points in the past as well as his attacking potential as he earned 89 points and 3 assists in just about 2300 minutes.

Midfielders

Bernard (owned by 0.5%, 6.5) –

Bernard will be hopeful to have a better second season in the Premier League following his first year with Everton. Last season, he showed hints of promise but generally failed to find his stride in attack with only 1 goal and 3 assists. He could still be a viable option, as Everton have extremely favorable early fixtures. With only 2 matches vs the top 6 in their first 14 fixtures of the season, Bernard could find plenty of points early in the season.

Mason Mount (owned by 0.5%, 6.0) –

Chelsea youngster Mason Mount could feature heavily this season for Chelsea and shows a lot of promise as a fantasy option priced at just 6.0. Following a successful loan spell to Vitesse in 2017 where he scored 9 goals in 29 appearances and a spell at Derby where he found the net 8 times in 35 matches. Having just signed a new long term contract July 15th, it is clear Mount will be in Lampard’s plans and could be a points machine if he gets the playing time he deserves.

Leandro Trossard (owned by 0.5%, 6.0) –

Another exciting new signing this new season is Leandro Trossard who will be looking to enhance Brighton’s attack. Trossard, who managed 14 goals and 7 assists while playing for Genk last season, is an exciting option considering he is priced at only 6.0. Although Brighton struggled last season, particularly in attack, Trossard could take set pieces for Brighton which could lead to a surplus of assist points.

Andriy Yarmolenko (owned by 0.4%, 6.0) –

After spending the majority of the last season injured, Yarmolenko will be keen on finding his way back into West Ham’s attack. Yarmolenko proved his ability to succeed last season with 2 goals early in the season but was ruled out for the remainder of the year. After the signing of Pablo Fornals, Yarmolenko could see his points ceiling heightened

Pascal Gross (owned by 0.8%, 6.5) –

The final midfielder in the team is Pascal Gross, Brighton’s main man in attack. Gross struggled with fitness last season and it was apparent in his attacking return, as he only managed 3 goals and 3 assists in 1864 minutes. Despite this, Gross has proved his ability in the Premier League before as he managed 164 points in the 17/18 season. After some solid performances near the end of last season, Gross could be one to watch at the start of this season.

Forwards

Billy Sharp (owned by 1.8%, 6.0) –

Sheffield United talisman Billy Sharp has been priced at a relatively low 6.0 this season after finding 23 goals and 4 assists in the Championship last season. Despite this, Sharp has only been selected by 1.8% of players this season. Sheffield are poised to retain their place in the Premier League this season, and Sharp could be key to their success.

Tammy Abraham (owned by 1.4%, 7.0) –

Tammy Abraham is the second Chelsea player to make the list, and one of the contenders to be Chelsea’s starting striker this season. Abraham found the net 25 times last season in the Championship while on loan from Chelsea to Aston Villa. Now, he will be competing with Olivier Giroud and Michy Batshuayi for the starting spot. If he succeeds in finding his place in the squad, Abraham could become one of the top scoring forwards this season.

Che Adams (owned by 1.3%, 6.0) –

Southampton new signing, Che Adams, could be a critical part of their squad this season. With 2 goals already in pre-season, Adams is off to a hot start in his Southampton career. In the Championship with Birmingham last season, Adams managed 22 goals and 4 assists and will be looking to carry that momentum into the Premier League. Adams is perhaps the best option at 6.0 and under.
